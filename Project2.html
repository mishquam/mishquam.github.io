Learning CAD

Learning TinkerCAD and Fuison 360

By Misha Patent

TinkerCAD: SHREK STOP

For my second project, I learned about CAD. To start I learned how to use TinkerCAD, and I combined a generic doorstop with a bust of Shrek, creating the "Shrek Stop."

Using TinkerCAD to manipulate 3D objects was easy and straightforward, however TinkerCAD's means of fabrication are less robust than Fusion360's.

The print completed without failure, and the final quality is acceptable to. In the future I will change my Prusa settings to have the first layer be more condenced.

The final product, though smaller than desired, functions as intended without flaw, and it currently resides in the engineering room.

Thingiverse Sources. Shrek Bust: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:287319 Door Stop: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:1241132

Fusion360: SHRAKE

Using Fusion360, I attached another Shrek head to the body of an articulated snake, to create a movable and flexable "Shrake"

Using Fusion360 to edit a mesh was much harder than in TinkerCAD. Ideally I would have used a mesh editor like Meshmiexer to make more precise plane cuts and to group and ungroup meshes. After some trial and error I was able to sucessfully add the Shrek head to the snake body.

While in Prusa Slicer, part of the mesh got dammaged and I had to use the "Fix Mesh" function to prevent the print from failing. The print succeeded and the final print quality was acceptable.
