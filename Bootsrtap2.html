3D Print Tests

An Analysis of a Simple Print and a Torture Test Print

By Misha Patent

NAMETAG

In the image above, you can see the STL of my nametag print and its settings in Prusa Slicer. The print was completed on a Prusa mini, using PLA. There was an initial issue with feeding the filament into the printer, which was resolved quickly.

Upon completion of the print, there were some noticeable features about the print. While the overall print was acceptable, slight stringing occurred between the letters on the nametag. Furthermore, the layers have some spacing between them, visible from the top and bottom of the nametag. The stringing and gasp in layers make me believe that. the printer’s nozzle is too high.

TORTURE TEST

After the nametag completed printing, I printed a “torture test” for the Prusa, designed to test the limits of the Prusa. Above is the STL of the torture test. Some noticeable features are the angle test, the cube, the verticality test, and the stringing test.

The torture test was completed without failing. However, some issues were present with the final build. There was significant stringing in the vertical test and the stringing test. Additionally, the printer couldn’t perfectly complete the angle test. Some odd filament issues also occurred on the cube and the bars. The main problem is that the nozzle is too high compared to the bed, causing the stringing on this print and my nametag.
